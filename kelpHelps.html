Kelp Helps

Kelp serves as a powerful tool in combatting climate change, excelling at capturing CO2 from the atmosphere.

Additionally, it offers a safe haven and living space for a diverse range of marine species, bolstering local biodiversity and promoting overall ecosystem well-being.

The development of bioplastics from kelp presents an opportunity to supplant conventional plastics, ultimately reducing plastic waste.

In summary, seaweeds like kelp have the capacity to greatly impact sustainable agriculture and aid in addressing pressing environmental issues like climate change, ocean acidification, and plastic pollution.
